THE JERRY GARCIA GUITAR METHOD - PART 2
transcribed by Maxxdaddy

Being a big fan of blues, I always liked the early Pigpen era dead. Jerry's approach to
the blues was generally lighthearted, with constant implications of the major third. These
examples give you an overview of Jerry's lively and articulate treatment of otherwise
simple tunes.

JERRY'S BLUES

1. MR. CHARLIE(EUROPE '72)
2. HARD TO HANDLE(BEAR'S CHOICE)
3. EASY WIND(WORKINGMAN'S DEAD)
4. AFTER MIDNIGHT

4. After Midnight
This is a good solo taken from a Jerry Garcia Band bootleg. It's 
played in E and the structure relies heavily on the melody for the phrasing
played over the (E7 G A)2x E7 G A B progression
